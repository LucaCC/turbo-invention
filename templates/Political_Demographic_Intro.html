County Primary Elections Project

Description: Every four years in the United States comes along a new general election where a president is elected. During that process primaries and caucases are held to determine what candidate is chosen per major party. This data set shows how each county in America voted for each candidate from both parties. These votes come from both priamries and caucases. All the data used comes from the CORGIS Dataset.

  1. Why do the percentages of each county not equal 100%?
    • The percentage for each county will equal 200% because the percentages are calculated by party.
  2. How many Republican and Democratic candidates recieved votes in the primaries respectively?
    • Republican's recieved nearly 30 million votes with Donald Trump recieving 14 million.
      The Democrats also recieved 30 million votes with Hillary Clinton recieving 17 million votes.
  3. Do independents get votes in the primaries?
    • No, only candidates from the two main parties, Republican and Democratic, are voted for during the primary elections.
  4. What is the difference between no preference and uncommited votes?
    • No preference is chosen when an electorate decides not to vote on that issue/candidate while uncommited has no dara in this data set and is useless.
  5. Why do North Dakota and Colorado have no data for republicans?
    • The GOP (Republican party) decided not to hold a vote in either of those states and instead held a party meeting to determin their delegate votes.
